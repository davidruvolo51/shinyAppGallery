1hr shiny challenge: runneR

One day, I decided to give myself a 1-hour shiny app challenge. I decided to create a shiny app to track my daily running habits (days run, monthly summary, distance, etc.) that can be accessed on any device. I also wanted to be reminded to enter data daily from a calendar event where I could click on a link to my shinyapps.io site. The challenge wasn't about writting code as fast as I could within 1 hour, it was about identifying areas of R/shiny programming that I am comfortable with and where there's room for growth.

Air Marshal Misconduct

In the ProPublica article TSA releases data on air marshal misconduct 7 years after we asked, Michael Grabell describes the lengthy journey to obtain the Air Marshal Misconduct dataset. The dataset contains 5213 records of misconduct from 2002 to 2012. Misconduct ranges from "Loss of Equipment" to "Criminal Arrest".

This shiny app visualizes the air marshall misconduct data released by ProPublica. The app was an experimentation with rendering R markdown files within a shiny app. This project was awarded a runner-up place in the data visualization category at the 2016 OxTalent competition. European Coffee Shop Finder

I'm a coffee drinker and I love to travel. When I lived in the UK, I traveled frequently and explored the local specialty roasters. The European Coffee Trip team creates incredible coffee travel guides to many cities around Europe. I've used this site before every trip and enjoyed wonderful cups of coffee in the most unique cafes.

I wanted to created an interactive map that would display all of the coffee shop in one view, and then could be used to plan daily itineraries and sightseeing breaks.

My Travel Adventures

I started using google maps in 2011 to track favorites places and plan trips. To date, I've logged 2189 of my favorite places in 32 countries across 4 continents. This shiny app is an interactive map of my travel adventures. This dataset is available on github here and the .R files can be found here.

If you'd like to explore your data, go to myactivity.google.com >> privacy >> control your content. I've also wrote a short guide to cleaning and processing the data. This could be handy if you haven't worked with json files before.

NFL Attendance Records

For the sports fans, here are the ESPN's NFL Attendance Records from 2009 to 2015 by team. Included are the number of games played by year (home, away, all combined) and attendance by game type (i.e., home, away, all combined). Attendance by team, year, and game type are recorded as mean and percent. 2009 is the first year where attendance data is available for all teams.

For the map making lovers, geo coordinates of each team's stadium are also available.
